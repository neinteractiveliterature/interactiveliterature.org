NELCO Conferences

The New England LARP Conference is a multi-tradition event focused on the craft of LARP. It is a weekend of LARP discussions, panels, workshops, and a LARP written during a Build Your Own Game seminar.

Over the years, many Intercons have also hosted panels and discussions. The "Thursday Thing" experiment started with Intercon J, adding a pre-convention extra day, with panels and workshops. The demand for more topics, more panels, and more workshops has grown as the Intercon attendance has grown. LARPers love to talk about LARP. Intercon, with its diverse crowd of attendees, is a great place to get a good mix of LARPers with a diverse set of experiences - and it now regularly schedules Thursday night and Friday day filled with panels and workshops.

But what if those people came together just to talk about LARP? NEIL thought this was a great experiment, in line with some of the other LARP conferences in the world. NELCO was born.

NELCO is a weekend where LARP designers, writers, GMs, players, costumers, gadgeteers, artists, and other creative people come together to talk about the theory and practice of LARP. There's always an eclectic mix of clever LARPers from different backgrounds, who love to talk about their hobby, their passion, and their experiences. Some of them get together for the weekend to create, write, and run a brand new LARP.

The most dangerous thought that you can have as a creative person is to think that you know what you're doing, because once you think you know what you're doing you stop looking around for other ways of doing things and you stop being able to see other ways of doing things. You become blind.

Bret Victor, "The Future of Programming", quoted in the LARP 101 BYOG seminar

We want to learn what you can teach us. We want to teach us what we know. This is how new and better LARP is born. Come join us at the next NELCO.
